Consumer Theory

Fundamental concepts in how consumers make choices under budget constraints

Overview

Consumer theory examines how individuals make decisions about what goods and services to purchase given their preferences and budget constraints. This forms the foundation for understanding demand and market behavior.

Key Concepts

Utility Functions

A mathematical representation of consumer preferences. Utility functions assign numerical values to different bundles of goods, where higher values indicate greater satisfaction.

Budget Constraint

The combination of goods a consumer can afford given their income and the prices of goods.

Marginal Rate of Substitution (MRS)

The rate at which a consumer is willing to trade one good for another while maintaining the same level of utility.

Optimal Choice

Consumers maximize utility where the marginal rate of substitution equals the price ratio (tangency condition).

Important Results

  • Income Effect: How consumption changes when income changes, holding prices constant
  • Substitution Effect: How consumption changes when relative prices change, holding utility constant
  • Normal vs. Inferior Goods: Normal goods see increased consumption with income; inferior goods see decreased consumption
  • Giffen Goods: Rare cases where demand increases as price increases (violates law of demand)

Practice Problems

  1. Given utility function U(x,y) = xy and budget constraint 2x + 3y = 60, find the optimal consumption bundle.
  2. If income doubles while all prices remain constant, what happens to the optimal bundle?
  3. Draw indifference curves for perfect substitutes and perfect complements.
  4. Explain why the MRS equals the price ratio at the optimal choice.
